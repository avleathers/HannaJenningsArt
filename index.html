ABOUT ME

My art is my way of sharing of the beauty I see in the world!

Welcome to my website. With each work of art I create, I ask "What exactly am I going for here?". I don't really know. But does anyone actually know?

Just like fashion, finding a style, or better, creating your own style takes a bit of time to develop. I began to watch hours upon hours of documentaries on the more well-known artists - Van Gogh, Francis Bacon, Edvard Munch, Matisse. Their use of colors and emotions really caught my attention. Van Gogh, the iconic depressed artist, used colors to make his paintings so vivid and lively. The world around him was a dark and lonely place so he chose to live within the borders of his canvases. The more I got to learn about these artists, the deeper I fell in the art world. The addiction had begun. From there I became more intrigued by less familiar artists. Some of my favorites right know are Barbara Kroll and Yvette Leihgeber. Isn't it weird how women are known for being more creative than men, yet in the art world, females get little credit and praise when it comes to art works. Think about it.

Anyway, their figures very much intrigued me. If you go to their instagrams, or just learn more about them, their art matches their personal aesthetic to a T! So right now, that’s what I’m seaking to find soon. Moving away from home was a big step in that journey…maybe it was the first step? I’ve sat down and thought about words and things that describe me or make me “Hanna.” What do I like and what do I not like? I’d like to think it’s a mixture of goth and glam? Punk and vintage? Something along those lines? Banksy, the 1975, Balenciaga, Supreme, Goodwill, Betsey Johnson, plaid, gold, boho, grunge, New York City, Paris—something like that. That's when I started looking into expanding my mediums past just oils, and take it even further. I am starting to include spray paints and inks to my paintings. I'm having a lot of fun with them and personally, I think it matches my personality. Artists always talk about putting yourself into your work so thats what my mission has been recently. I'm an INFJ which means opening up to people isn't really my thing. I'm pretty introverted and have a really hard time small talking and expressing who I actually am to people that I don't know very well. Because of this, I've turned towards art to hopefully show people a version of me that I can't fully express with words.
